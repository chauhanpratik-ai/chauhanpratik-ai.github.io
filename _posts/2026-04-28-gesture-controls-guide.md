One of the most loved features in VX Player is its **intuitive gesture control system**. Once you get used to it, you'll never want to go back to tapping tiny on-screen buttons. Here's a complete guide to every gesture available.

## Swipe Gestures Overview

VX Player divides the screen into zones, each controlling a different parameter with a vertical or horizontal swipe.

### 🔆 Brightness — Left Side Vertical Swipe

Swipe **up** on the **left half** of the screen to increase brightness.  
Swipe **down** on the **left half** to decrease brightness.

A brightness indicator appears briefly on screen as you adjust. VX Player remembers your brightness setting per session so you don't have to re-adjust every time.

### 🔊 Volume — Right Side Vertical Swipe

Swipe **up** on the **right half** of the screen to raise the volume.  
Swipe **down** on the **right half** to lower the volume.

The system volume level is shown on screen as you swipe, giving you precise control without hunting for hardware buttons.

### ⏩ Seek — Horizontal Swipe

Swipe **left or right** anywhere on the screen to seek backward or forward through the video.

- A fast short swipe moves a few seconds.
- A long slow swipe jumps further ahead or back.

You can customise seek intervals to **5s, 10s, 15s, or 30s** in Settings → Playback Controls.

### 🔍 Pinch to Zoom

Place two fingers on the screen and **pinch outward** to zoom in on the video, or **pinch inward** to zoom out. This is great for zooming in on subtitles or getting a closer look at a scene.

To quickly switch between standard aspect ratios (fit, fill, stretch, zoom), tap the aspect ratio icon in the top bar instead.

## Double-Tap Controls

| Gesture | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Double-tap left side | Rewind by seek interval |
| Double-tap right side | Fast-forward by seek interval |
| Double-tap centre | Play / Pause |

## Screen & Controls Lock

Accidentally tapping the screen mid-movie is annoying. Use the **lock button** (padlock icon) to disable all touch controls. Tap the lock icon again to unlock.

This is especially useful when handing your phone to someone or when lying down watching a video.

## Tips & Tricks

- **Gesture sensitivity** can be adjusted in Settings if the default feels too sensitive or not responsive enough.
- Combine **background playback** with gesture controls — even on the lock screen you get playback controls.
- **Auto-rotate** follows your device orientation, but you can lock it to portrait or landscape manually using the rotate icon during playback.
