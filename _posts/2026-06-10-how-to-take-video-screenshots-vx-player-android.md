## 🎯 How to Capture a Frame in VX Player

**Step 1 — Find your frame**

1. Open the video in VX Player
2. Use the **seek bar** or **arrow buttons** to get close to the frame you want
3. For precise control, tap the **−0.1s** or **+0.1s** step buttons to move one frame at a time

**Step 2 — Pause on the exact frame**

- Tap **Pause** (or press Space with a keyboard)
- Use the **frame-step button** (‹‹ / ›› near the play button) for single-frame precision

**Step 3 — Capture**

1. Tap the screen to show the control overlay
2. Tap the **camera icon** (📷) in the top control bar
3. A brief notification confirms: *"Screenshot saved to gallery"*

Done — the frame is saved at the video's native resolution.

---

## 📂 Where Screenshots Are Saved

Screenshots are saved to:

```
Internal Storage → Pictures → VXPlayer
```

They also appear in your **Photos / Gallery app** under a dedicated *VX Player Screenshots* album, making them easy to find, share, or set as wallpaper.

---

## 🖼️ Screenshot Quality

VX Player saves screenshots as **JPEG** (default) or **PNG**:

- Go to **Settings → Player → Screenshot Format**
- Choose **PNG** for lossless quality (larger file size)
- Choose **JPEG** for smaller files (slight compression)

The saved resolution matches the **source video resolution** — a 1080p video produces a 1920×1080 screenshot; a 4K video produces a 3840×2160 image.

---

## ⏩ Burst Capture — Save Multiple Frames

For capturing a sequence of frames (useful for studying animations or slide presentations):

1. Tap the camera icon **repeatedly** while the video advances, or
2. Enable **Auto Capture** in advanced settings: set an interval (e.g., every 5 seconds) and VX Player saves frames automatically during playback
