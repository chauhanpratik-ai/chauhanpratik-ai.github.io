Modern video files — particularly MKV containers — routinely contain multiple audio tracks. A single film file might include the original English audio, a French dub, a Spanish dub, and an audio description track for visually impaired viewers, all in one file. Choosing between them should be instant and seamless.

**VX Player** detects and displays all audio tracks in a file, letting you switch between them mid-playback with a single tap — no restart required.

## 🎵 What Are Multiple Audio Tracks?

A video file with multiple audio tracks is like a book with several translations bound together. The video track is shared; only the audio changes depending on your selection.

| Container Format | Multiple Audio Tracks | Max Tracks |
|---|---|---|
| MKV | ✅ Full support | Unlimited |
| MP4 | ✅ Yes | Up to ~16 |
| AVI | ⚠️ Limited | 2 typically |
| MOV | ✅ Yes | Multiple |
| TS (MPEG-2) | ✅ Yes | Multiple |
| VOB (DVD) | ✅ Yes | Multiple |

MKV is the go-to format for multi-audio content because it places no practical limit on the number of tracks.

## 🌐 Setting a Default Audio Language

If you always prefer a specific language, VX Player can auto-select it:

1. Go to **Settings → Player → Preferred Audio Language**
2. Enter the language code (e.g. `eng` for English, `jpn` for Japanese, `fra` for French)
3. Whenever a video has a matching track, VX Player selects it automatically

This is especially useful for anime fans who always want Japanese audio, or for viewers who always prefer their native language dub over the original.

## 📊 Audio Delay Adjustment

If a specific audio track is slightly out of sync with the video (common in poorly encoded files), VX Player includes an audio delay control:

1. During playback, open the player menu
2. Find **Audio Delay** or **A/V Sync**
3. Use the slider: negative values advance audio, positive values delay it
4. Adjust in 100ms increments until audio aligns with video

| Symptom | Adjustment Needed |
|---|---|
| Audio slightly ahead of video | Add positive delay (+200ms, +300ms) |
| Audio slightly behind video | Set negative delay (-200ms, -300ms) |
| Audio severely out of sync | Check if wrong audio track selected |

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How do I switch between audio languages in VX Player on Android?
Tap the screen during playback to show controls, then tap the audio track icon (headphones symbol). A list of all available audio tracks appears. Tap your preferred language or track, and VX Player switches immediately without restarting the video.

### 2. Which video formats support multiple audio tracks in VX Player?
MKV (Matroska) has the best multi-audio support with unlimited tracks. MP4, MOV, TS, and VOB also support multiple audio streams. AVI has limited support, typically only two tracks. For multi-language content, MKV is the recommended format.

### 3. Can VX Player automatically select my preferred language when a video has multiple tracks?
Yes. Go to VX Player Settings → Player → Preferred Audio Language and enter your language code (e.g. "eng" for English, "jpn" for Japanese). VX Player will automatically select a matching track when one is available.

### 4. Does VX Player support high-quality audio codecs like DTS-HD and TrueHD from Blu-ray rips?
Yes. VX Player supports a full range of audio codecs including AC3, DTS, DTS-HD MA, TrueHD/Atmos, FLAC, and AAC. All audio track types commonly found in MKV Blu-ray rips are supported.

### 5. What if the audio track I want is out of sync with the video in VX Player?
Use VX Player's Audio Delay control, found in the player menu during playback. Adjust the delay in milliseconds — negative values shift audio earlier, positive values shift it later. Adjust until the audio aligns correctly with the video.
