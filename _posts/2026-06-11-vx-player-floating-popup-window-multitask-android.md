You're halfway through a video tutorial when a message arrives that needs a reply. Or you want to watch a lecture while taking notes. With **VX Player's floating popup window**, the video keeps playing in a small overlay while you freely use any other app — no interruptions, no pausing, no losing your place.

---

## 🪟 Two Floating Modes Explained

VX Player offers two ways to keep video visible while multitasking:

| Mode | How it works | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Picture-in-Picture (PiP)** | System-level floating window, managed by Android OS | Universal multitasking — works over any app |
| **Floating Window** | VX Player's own draggable overlay | When you want more control over position and size |

---

## ▶️ Enabling Picture-in-Picture (PiP)

PiP is the quickest way to float a video:

1. Start playing any video in VX Player
2. Press the **Android Home button** (or swipe up from the bottom on gesture navigation)
3. VX Player automatically shrinks into a small floating window
4. **Drag it** to any corner of your screen
5. Use any other app normally — the video keeps playing

**Controls inside PiP window:**
- Tap the window → shows **Play/Pause** and **Close** buttons briefly
- Double-tap → returns to fullscreen
- Drag → repositions the window
- Pinch (Android 12+) → resizes the window

---

## ⚙️ Configuring PiP Behaviour

1. In VX Player, go to **Settings → Player → Picture-in-Picture**
2. Options include:
   - **Enable PiP on Home press** (on/off)
   - **Default PiP size**: Small / Medium / Large
   - **Auto-enter PiP**: automatically trigger when switching apps

---

## 🔧 Enabling the Floating Window Mode

For VX Player's own draggable overlay (separate from system PiP):

1. During playback, tap the screen to show controls
2. Tap the **floating window icon** (looks like a small window with an arrow)
3. The video appears as a **resizable draggable window** on top of the launcher or other apps
4. Drag the corners to resize; drag the title bar to reposition

> **Note:** Floating window requires the "Display over other apps" permission. VX Player will prompt you to grant this the first time.

---

## 🖐️ Gesture Controls Inside the Floating Window

Even in the small floating window, key gestures still work:

| Gesture | Action |
|---|---|
| Tap window | Show / hide playback controls |
| Double-tap | Play / Pause |
| Swipe left/right on window | Seek backward / forward |
| Long-press and drag | Move window position |
| Pinch (Android 12+) | Resize window |

## 🔋 Battery & Performance

The floating window uses the same hardware decoder as fullscreen mode — there's no extra battery cost. On devices with AMOLED displays, the dark areas of the video use near-zero power, making small floating windows especially efficient for dark-themed content.
