You're watching a film shot with quiet dialogue, or playing a video where the recorded volume is simply too low. The system volume is already at maximum. This is where **VX Player's Audio Boost** steps in — it applies digital amplification to push the audio well beyond the standard 100% ceiling, giving you noticeably louder playback without switching apps or rooting your device.

## 🔊 How Audio Boost Works

Audio boost applies **digital gain** to the decoded audio stream before it reaches your speakers or headphones. Unlike the hardware volume buttons which control the device's output stage, audio boost amplifies the signal inside the app — which means it works independently of your system volume and can produce more output than the hardware limit would otherwise allow.

| Volume setting | What it means |
|---|---|
| 0–100% | Normal device volume range |
| 100–150% | Moderate boost — louder dialogue, clearer sound |
| 150–200% | Maximum boost — significant amplification, risk of clipping on very loud content |

---

## ▶️ Enabling Audio Boost During Playback

1. Open any video in VX Player
2. Tap the screen to reveal the playback controls
3. Tap the **audio icon** or **settings gear** at the top of the screen
4. Select **Audio Boost**
5. Drag the **boost slider** to your desired level (100%–200%)
6. Tap outside the panel to dismiss — boost is now active

The boost level is shown as an indicator on-screen while active.

---

## 🎚️ Using the Equalizer for Better Sound

Audio Boost raises the overall volume; the **Equalizer** shapes the frequency response for better tonal quality:

1. During playback, tap **Audio** → **Equalizer**
2. Choose a preset or tap **Custom**:

| Preset | Best for |
|---|---|
| Normal | Balanced — default starting point |
| Bass Boost | Action movies, music, low-end emphasis |
| Rock | Guitar-heavy content, punchy mids |
| Classical | Orchestral, open soundstage |
| Vocal Enhance | Dialogue-heavy films, interviews |
| Treble Boost | Crisp highs, detailed ambient sound |

3. In Custom mode, drag each frequency slider:
   - **60 Hz** — deep bass / bass rumble
   - **230 Hz** — upper bass / body
   - **910 Hz** — midrange / presence
   - **3 kHz** — upper mids / vocals
   - **14 kHz** — air / sparkle

## 🎧 Audio Boost with Headphones vs Speakers

| Output | Recommended Boost |
|---|---|
| Phone speaker | 110–140% (avoid distortion) |
| Bluetooth speaker | 130–160% (depends on speaker quality) |
| Wired headphones | 130–170% (comfortable for most) |
| High-impedance headphones | 150–200% (these handle more power) |

> **Tip:** Combine Vocal Enhance equalizer preset with 130% boost for dramatically clearer dialogue in foreign language films or low-budget recordings.

---

## 🔇 Delay Audio Sync Fix

If boosting audio reveals a lip-sync issue (common with poorly encoded files), fix it inside the same audio panel:

1. **Audio → Audio Delay**
2. Adjust delay in milliseconds: positive value delays audio, negative value advances it
3. Most sync issues are fixed within ±200ms
