## 🎵 Why Video Players Need an Equalizer

Your phone's speaker or headphone output is not flat. Different hardware has different frequency response characteristics:

- **Phone speakers** typically boost mids (for voice clarity) and roll off deep bass  
- **Earbuds** vary widely — cheap buds often emphasise harsh highs  
- **Over-ear headphones** may have a V-shaped response with exaggerated bass and treble  
- **Bluetooth speakers** add their own colouration  

An equalizer lets you compensate for your hardware's characteristics and the specific content you're watching. The result: audio that sounds the way the creator intended, on your specific device.

## 🥁 Bass Boost — The Fast Track to Bigger Sound

For a quick impact without tuning individual bands, VX Player's dedicated **Bass Boost** control is your shortcut:

1. In the Audio Effects/Equalizer menu, find the **Bass Boost** slider  
2. Slide it to 0–100 (strength level)  
3. Start around 30–50 for a noticeable but not overwhelming boost  
4. For movies and action content: 60–80 is excellent on headphones  

> ⚠️ On phone speakers, excessive bass boost (above 70) can cause distortion since small speakers physically cannot reproduce deep bass. Keep bass boost moderate for speaker use.

## 🔁 Virtualizer — Wider Soundstage on Headphones

VX Player also includes a **Virtualizer** (sometimes called 3D Audio or Surround):

1. In the Audio Effects menu, find **Virtualizer** or **3D Sound**  
2. Enable it and set the strength (0–100)  
3. On headphones, this creates a wider, more spacious soundstage  
4. Excellent for surround-encoded films and concert footage  

> 💡 The Virtualizer works best with stereo headphones. On phone speakers or mono Bluetooth, the effect is minimal or non-existent.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Does VX Player's equalizer work with Bluetooth headphones?
Yes. VX Player's equalizer processes audio before it's sent to any output, including Bluetooth headphones. The EQ settings apply regardless of whether you're using wired earphones, wireless headphones, a Bluetooth speaker, or your phone's built-in speaker.

### 2. What is the difference between the equalizer and the bass boost in VX Player?
The equalizer gives precise control over 10 specific frequency bands across the full audio spectrum. The bass boost is a simpler single-slider control that amplifies low frequencies with a single adjustment. For quick tuning use bass boost; for detailed control use the full EQ.

### 3. Will boosting the bass too much damage my phone speakers?
Very high bass boost levels can cause distortion and place extra stress on small phone speakers, but modern Android phones have built-in audio limits that prevent damage. For comfortable playback without distortion, keep bass boost below 60–70 on phone speakers.

### 4. Does the equalizer affect video audio differently than music?
No — the equalizer affects all audio played through VX Player equally, regardless of whether it's a movie soundtrack, a music video, or a podcast. The same EQ profile applies to any content playing in the app.

### 5. Can I turn off the equalizer quickly without losing my settings?
Yes. The equalizer has an **Enable/Disable** toggle switch at the top of the EQ screen. Toggling it off bypasses all EQ processing while preserving your slider positions. Toggle it back on to restore your settings instantly.
