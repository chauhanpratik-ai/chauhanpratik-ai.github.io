## 🎬 All-Format Playback — No Conversions Needed

The most frustrating experience with budget video players is downloading a movie only to find it "unsupported." VX Player eliminates this problem entirely. It natively supports every major format:

- **MP4, M4V, MOV** — Standard HD video from phones, cameras, and streaming downloads  
- **MKV** — The container of choice for Blu-ray rips and high-quality encodes  
- **AVI, WMV, FLV** — Legacy formats from older devices and the web  
- **WebM, OGV** — Open-source web formats  
- **TS, MTS, M2TS** — Camcorder and broadcast formats  

Hardware-accelerated decoding means VX Player offloads video processing to your device's dedicated GPU, keeping playback smooth even on mid-range phones. Unlike software-only decoders that drain battery and cause stuttering, VX Player's hardware decoder maintains a steady framerate on 4K content without breaking a sweat.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** If a file doesn't play smoothly, go to **Settings → Decoder** and switch between Hardware (HW) and Software (SW) decoding to find what works best for your device.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Is VX Player really free?
Yes. VX Player is 100% free to download and use on the Google Play Store. All features — including 4K playback, IPTV support, equalizer, and Chromecast — are available without any paid upgrade or subscription.

### 2. What video formats does VX Player support?
VX Player supports all major formats including MP4, MKV, AVI, MOV, FLV, WMV, WebM, TS, MTS, M2TS, and more. It also handles H.264, H.265/HEVC, VP8, VP9, and AV1 codecs.

### 3. Does VX Player work on Android TV?
VX Player is designed for Android smartphones and tablets. While it may sideload on some Android TV devices, it is optimised for touch-based Android interfaces rather than remote-control navigation.

### 4. Can VX Player play videos from Google Drive or Dropbox?
VX Player supports direct URL streaming. You can generate a direct download link from Google Drive or Dropbox and paste it into the Network Stream feature to play cloud-stored videos.

### 5. How does VX Player compare to VLC for Android?
Both are excellent free players, but VX Player has a cleaner modern UI, built-in equalizer, a better file manager, and smoother gesture controls. VLC has a longer track record and wider codec coverage through its software decoder, making both strong choices for different users.
