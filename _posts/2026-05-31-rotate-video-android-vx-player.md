Video rotation is one of those problems that sounds trivial but causes genuine frustration: a video recorded in portrait mode opens sideways on your phone, or a landscape film keeps switching to portrait every time your wrist moves slightly. VX Player gives you complete control over screen orientation during playback.

## 📱 Understanding Screen Rotation Modes

VX Player offers four distinct rotation modes:

| Mode | Behaviour | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Auto Rotate** | Follows device orientation sensor | General use, comfortable viewing |
| **Lock Landscape** | Forces landscape regardless of tilt | Films, TV shows, widescreen content |
| **Lock Portrait** | Forces portrait regardless of tilt | Vertical videos, phone video recordings |
| **Lock Reverse Landscape** | 180° landscape for cable management | Charging in landscape, desk use |

## ⚙️ How to Control Rotation in VX Player

### During Playback

1. Tap the screen to show the player controls
2. Look for the **rotation lock icon** (a screen with rotation arrows)
3. Tap to cycle through modes: Auto → Lock Landscape → Lock Portrait
4. The screen adjusts immediately

### From Settings

1. VX Player → ⚙️ **Settings**
2. Navigate to **Display** or **Player Settings**
3. Find **Screen Orientation**
4. Choose your preferred default mode

## 🔒 Orientation Lock — Stop Accidental Rotation

The most common use for rotation lock is preventing accidental rotation during playback:

**Scenario**: You're watching a film in bed. Every time you shift position, the phone rotates and you lose your comfortable viewing angle.

**Solution**: Lock to Landscape. The video stays in landscape mode regardless of how you tilt the phone.

| Trigger | Without Lock | With Lock |
|---|---|---|
| Rotating phone while lying down | Screen rotates, disrupts viewing | Screen stays locked |
| Passing phone to someone else | Screen may rotate | Screen stays locked |
| Using phone on side for cables | Constant rotation changes | Screen locked as set |

## 📹 Fixing Videos Recorded in the Wrong Orientation

Smartphones occasionally save videos with incorrect rotation metadata — the video plays sideways or upside down even though it looks correct in the gallery. VX Player handles this in two ways:

### Temporary Fix (In-App Rotation)
1. During playback, open the player menu
2. Find **Video Rotation** or **Rotate Video**
3. Tap to rotate the video 90° clockwise
4. Tap again for 180°, again for 270°, again to reset to 0°

This rotates the video frame within the player window for the current session.

### Permanent Fix
Some video player apps (including desktop tools like HandBrake) can re-encode or update the metadata to permanently fix the rotation. VX Player's in-app rotation works as a real-time display fix without modifying the source file.

## 🖥️ Auto-Rotate with Sensor Calibration

If VX Player's auto-rotate feels sluggish or triggers at wrong angles, the issue is usually the device's accelerometer sensitivity:

1. **Test system rotation**: Does your Android homescreen rotate smoothly? If not, the sensor may need calibration.
2. **Android Settings → Display → Rotate screen**: Ensure system rotation is enabled
3. **In VX Player**: Set rotation to Auto to use the system sensor

Some Android devices (notably older Xiaomi and Samsung) have sticky rotation sensors. Waving the phone in a figure-8 pattern a few times recalibrates the accelerometer.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. How do I lock the screen to landscape in VX Player while watching a video?
Tap the screen during playback to show the controls, then tap the rotation lock icon to cycle to Lock Landscape mode. The screen will stay in landscape orientation regardless of how you tilt your device.

### 2. My video plays sideways in VX Player — how do I fix it?
During playback, open the player menu and find the Video Rotation option. Tap to rotate 90° clockwise. Tap again if needed (180° or 270°) until the video displays correctly.

### 3. Can VX Player automatically detect and correct video orientation?
VX Player reads the rotation metadata embedded in the video file. If the metadata is correct, playback will be in the right orientation automatically. If the file has incorrect metadata, use the in-player rotation option to manually correct it.

### 4. Why does auto-rotate keep triggering accidentally in VX Player?
Auto-rotate uses your device's accelerometer. If you're lying down or moving, minor tilts can trigger rotation. Switch to Lock Landscape or Lock Portrait mode during playback to prevent accidental rotation while still keeping system auto-rotate enabled for other apps.

### 5. Does VX Player support reverse landscape mode for charging in landscape?
Yes. VX Player includes a Lock Reverse Landscape mode that rotates the display 180° from standard landscape. This is useful when your phone is on a wireless charger or desk stand that positions the cable on the right side.
