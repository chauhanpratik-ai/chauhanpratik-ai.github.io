A tablet transforms video watching — 10–13 inches of screen, stereo speakers, and enough computing power to handle 4K files without breaking a sweat. **VX Player is built to take full advantage of every inch**, with a tablet-responsive UI, landscape-first design, and productivity features like keyboard shortcuts and multi-window support.

---

## 📐 Tablet-Optimised Layout

VX Player automatically detects larger screens and switches to:

| Feature | Phone | Tablet |
|---|---|---|
| Library grid columns | 2 | 3–4 |
| Folder browser | List view | Grid view |
| Playback controls | Bottom bar | Spread across screen |
| Subtitle position | Bottom 10% | Adjustable from edges |
| Touch zones | Narrow | Wide — less mis-taps |

The result is a library that feels like a proper media centre dashboard rather than a scaled-up phone app.

---

## 🖥️ Landscape-First Playback

Tablets are naturally held landscape, and VX Player defaults to landscape during playback. Key tablet advantages:

- **Wider gesture zones** — swipe volume on the right third, brightness on the left third; the zones are proportionally larger on a tablet, making accidental triggers rare
- **More screen space for subtitles** — subtitles sit comfortably below the video without overlapping faces
- **Larger seek bar** — precise scrubbing across a 10-inch bar is far easier than on a 6-inch phone

---

## ⌨️ Keyboard Shortcuts (Tablets with Keyboard Cover)

When a Bluetooth keyboard, USB-C keyboard, or keyboard cover is connected:

| Key | Action |
|---|---|
| **Space** | Play / Pause |
| **← →** | Seek back / forward 10 seconds |
| **↑ ↓** | Volume up / down |
| **M** | Mute toggle |
| **F** | Toggle fullscreen |
| **S** | Next subtitle track |
| **A** | Next audio track |
| **N** | Next video in playlist |

These shortcuts are especially useful for tablet users working through a video course or presentation recording.

## 🪟 Multi-Window & Split Screen

**Split Screen (Android 7.0+):**
1. Open VX Player
2. Long-press the recent apps button
3. Select **Split Screen**
4. Choose a second app (browser, notes, messaging) for the other half
5. VX Player continues playing in one half while you work in the other

**Picture-in-Picture:**
Press the home button while playing — VX Player collapses to a floating window you can drag anywhere on screen. Works on all Android 8.0+ devices.

---

## 🔋 4K Playback on Tablet

Higher-end tablets (Samsung Galaxy Tab S series, Xiaomi Pad 6+, OnePlus Pad) support hardware-decoded 4K HEVC and AV1. VX Player routes playback through the hardware decoder automatically:

| Tablet chip | 4K HEVC | 4K AV1 |
|---|---|---|
| Snapdragon 8 Gen 2/3 | ✅ | ✅ |
| Dimensity 9000+ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Snapdragon 7s Gen 2 | ✅ | ⚠️ (SW fallback) |
| Budget chips (6125, etc.) | ⚠️ (SW, may drop frames) | ❌ |

If 4K content stutters, go to **Settings → Player → Decoder** and try switching between **Auto**, **HW**, and **SW** to find what your specific chip handles best.

---

## 🔊 Stereo & External Audio

Most tablets have front-facing stereo speakers — a major upgrade from phone speakers. VX Player's audio pipeline sends the full stereo mix without down-mixing, preserving the stereo image. Pair this with the built-in equalizer's **Wide Stereo** preset for a noticeably more immersive sound from your tablet's speakers.

For external audio: connect Bluetooth speakers or headphones; VX Player switches output automatically.
