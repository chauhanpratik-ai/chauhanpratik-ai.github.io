## 🎨 User Interface

**VX Player** was designed from the ground up for modern Android. The dark AMOLED-friendly interface keeps controls out of the way until you need them. Navigation is intuitive — swipe up from the library to browse, tap once to play.

**MX Player** built its reputation in the early Android era when it was the only player that handled hardware decoding reliably. Its UI has aged poorly — the home screen is cluttered with promoted content and ads in the free version.

**VLC** prioritises function over form. It's open-source and reliable, but its mobile UI often feels like an afterthought. It can feel overwhelming for casual users.

**Winner: VX Player** — cleanest, most modern mobile experience.

## 🎬 Codec & Format Support

All three players handle the everyday formats (MP4, MKV, AVI) without issue. Where they differ:

| Format | VX Player | MX Player | VLC |
|---|---|---|---|
| H.264 / AVC | ✅ HW + SW | ✅ HW + SW | ✅ HW + SW |
| H.265 / HEVC | ✅ HW preferred | ✅ HW | ✅ HW + SW |
| AV1 | ✅ HW (Android 10+) | ⚠️ SW only | ✅ SW |
| VP9 | ✅ HW | ✅ HW | ✅ HW |
| MKV containers | ✅ Full | ✅ Full | ✅ Full |
| ISO / Blu-ray disc images | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ VLC only |
| Rare codecs (RealMedia, etc.) | ❌ | ⚠️ With plugin | ✅ VLC only |

For everyday viewing including 4K HEVC and AV1 content, VX Player and MX Player are both excellent. VLC wins on niche codec breadth.

## ⚡ Performance

VX Player's hardware acceleration pipeline is tightly optimised for modern Qualcomm and MediaTek chipsets. On a mid-range device playing a 4K HEVC file, VX Player consistently runs cooler and uses less battery than MX Player's equivalent.

MX Player introduced hardware decoding to Android years ago and it remains solid, but the app's background processes and ad network inflate memory usage.

VLC uses its own software decoder libraries by default, which gives it unmatched compatibility but costs CPU cycles. On budget phones, VLC can drop frames where VX Player handles the same file flawlessly.

## 🔒 Privacy Features

Only **VX Player** includes a dedicated private folder with app lock — a passcode-protected space for videos you don't want appearing in the regular gallery. Neither MX Player nor VLC offer this.
