# 📡 How to Cast Videos from Android to TV Using VX Player

There's nothing quite like watching your favourite film or series on a big screen. With VX Player's built-in **Chromecast support**, you can wirelessly beam any video from your Android phone or tablet directly to your TV — no cables, no HDMI adapters, no extra apps. In minutes, your small-screen video becomes a proper home cinema experience.

This guide walks you through exactly how to cast videos from Android to TV using VX Player, what hardware you need, troubleshooting tips, and how to use your phone as a remote while casting.

---

## 📺 What You Need to Cast Videos to TV

Before you start, make sure you have:

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| Android phone/tablet | Android 5.0 or later with VX Player installed |
| Chromecast device | Chromecast (any generation), Chromecast with Google TV, or Android TV with built-in Cast |
| Same Wi-Fi network | Both your phone and Chromecast must be on the same network |
| VX Player | Latest version from the Google Play Store |

> 💡 **No Chromecast?** Many smart TVs from Sony, Philips, and TCL have Google Cast built in. Check your TV's remote for a **Cast** icon or check the manufacturer's app compatibility list.

---

## 🔌 Setting Up Chromecast (If You Haven't Already)

If your Chromecast is already set up and working with YouTube or Netflix, skip this section. If it's new:

1. Plug the Chromecast into your TV's HDMI port  
2. Download the **Google Home** app on your Android phone  
3. Follow the in-app setup wizard to connect Chromecast to your Wi-Fi  
4. Give your Chromecast a name (e.g. "Living Room TV")  
5. Switch your TV input to the correct HDMI port  

Setup takes about 5 minutes and only needs to be done once.

## 📲 How to Cast a Video from VX Player — Step by Step

### Step 1: Connect to the Same Wi-Fi
Ensure your Android device is connected to the same Wi-Fi network as your Chromecast. This is the most common source of casting failures.

### Step 2: Open VX Player and Select Your Video
Launch VX Player, browse to your video using the built-in file manager, and tap the file to begin playback.

### Step 3: Tap the Cast Icon
During playback, tap the screen to reveal controls. Look for the **Cast icon** (a rectangle with Wi-Fi waves in the corner) in the top area of the player interface. Tap it.

### Step 4: Select Your Chromecast
A list of available Cast devices on your network appears. Tap your Chromecast or Android TV name.

### Step 5: Enjoy on the Big Screen
The video transfers to your TV and begins playing. Your phone becomes the remote — control playback, seek, adjust volume, and switch audio tracks directly from VX Player on your phone.

## 🎛️ Controlling Playback While Casting

Once cast is active, your phone screen shows the VX Player cast control interface:

- **▶ / ⏸ Play / Pause** — Tap the play/pause button  
- **⏪ ⏩ Seek** — Drag the progress bar or use the ± 10s skip buttons  
- **🔊 Volume** — Use the hardware volume buttons on your phone  
- **🎵 Audio Track** — Tap the audio icon to switch languages  
- **🔤 Subtitles** — Tap CC to enable/disable subtitles  
- **⏹ Stop Casting** — Tap the Cast icon again and select "Stop Casting"  

> 💡 You can minimise VX Player and use other apps on your phone while the video plays on the TV. The cast session continues uninterrupted.

## 🌐 Casting Network Streams and IPTV to TV

VX Player's Chromecast support isn't limited to local files. You can also cast:

- **Direct URL streams** — Paste an HTTP/HTTPS video URL and cast it to the TV  
- **IPTV channels** — Load an M3U playlist, select a channel, and cast  
- **YouTube-compatible links** — Many direct video URLs work seamlessly  

This makes VX Player a versatile IPTV-to-TV bridge, letting you watch online channels on your TV without a smart TV subscription or a separate IPTV box.

## 📶 Tips for the Best Casting Quality

**Use 5 GHz Wi-Fi when possible.** The 2.4 GHz band is more congested and prone to interference. Connect both your phone and Chromecast to the 5 GHz band for lower latency and fewer buffering interruptions.

**Keep your phone close to the router while casting.** Distance and walls weaken the Wi-Fi signal. A strong, stable connection ensures smooth 4K casting.

**Cast via the local network, not the internet.** When you cast a local file, VX Player streams it from your phone to the Chromecast over your home network — the file never leaves your local network. This ensures the maximum quality and speed.

**Use VX Player's built-in subtitle support during casting.** Subtitles loaded in VX Player render on the TV screen as well during a cast session. Load your .SRT file before starting the cast.

## ⚠️ Common Casting Problems and Solutions

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Cast icon doesn't appear | Confirm phone and Chromecast are on the same Wi-Fi network |
| Chromecast not listed | Restart the Chromecast and refresh the cast device list |
| Video plays on phone but not TV | Close and reopen VX Player; try selecting the Chromecast again |
| Video stutters on TV | Switch to a 5 GHz Wi-Fi band; reduce video resolution if streaming |
| No audio on TV | Check TV and Chromecast volume; ensure audio track is set in VX Player |
| Casting disconnects randomly | Wi-Fi signal instability — move router closer or use a Wi-Fi extender |

## 🔄 Alternative: Mirror Your Entire Screen

If VX Player's native cast doesn't work with a specific file type, Android's **screen mirroring** feature is a fallback:

1. Swipe down the notification shade and tap **Cast** or **Smart View**  
2. Select your Chromecast or smart TV  
3. Your entire phone screen is mirrored — open VX Player and play your video  

Screen mirroring introduces slightly more latency and reduces battery life faster than native casting, so native casting through VX Player is always preferred.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Do I need a Chromecast to cast from VX Player?
You need a Google Cast-compatible receiver. This includes Google Chromecast devices, Chromecast with Google TV, and smart TVs from Sony, TCL, Philips, and others that have Google Cast built in. Android TV devices also support casting.

### 2. Can I cast 4K video from VX Player to my TV?
Yes, provided your Chromecast supports 4K (Chromecast Ultra, Chromecast with Google TV 4K) and your TV is 4K capable. The quality of the cast depends on your Wi-Fi speed and the encoding of the source file.

### 3. Will VX Player cast to a non-Google TV like a Fire TV Stick?
VX Player uses Google Cast protocol. Amazon Fire TV Stick uses a different protocol. To cast to a Fire TV Stick, you would need to use Android's screen mirroring feature instead of VX Player's native cast button.

### 4. Can I continue using my phone while casting?
Yes. Once casting begins, you can minimise VX Player or use other apps. The video continues playing on your TV. The VX Player playback controls remain accessible from the notification shade.

### 5. Does casting use mobile data?
No. Casting over your home Wi-Fi network uses local data only and does not consume mobile data. All communication happens between your phone and the Chromecast over your home network.
