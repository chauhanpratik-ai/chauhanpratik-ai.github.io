There are moments when you're deep into a documentary, a sports match, or a lecture video and someone texts you. You pause, reply, lose your flow, and return to the video. Repeat this five times and the experience is ruined. **Picture-in-Picture (PiP) mode** solves this completely — your video shrinks into a floating window that stays on screen while you do everything else on your phone.

VX Player for Android supports PiP natively, with a clean floating window you can reposition, resize, and dismiss instantly. This guide covers how to enable and use PiP mode in VX Player, tips for the best experience, and what to do if PiP isn't working on your device.

## 🖼️ What Is Picture-in-Picture (PiP) Mode?

Picture-in-Picture is an Android feature (available since Android 8.0 Oreo) that allows a video app to display a small floating window on top of other apps. Think of it as a mini-screen that follows you around:

- Watch a cooking video while browsing the recipe ingredient list  
- Follow a tutorial while taking notes in another app  
- Keep a sports stream visible while messaging friends  
- Listen and watch a lecture while browsing course materials  

| PiP Capability in VX Player | Details |
|---|---|
| Minimum Android version | Android 8.0 (Oreo) |
| Window shape | Rectangular, rounded corners |
| Repositioning | Drag anywhere on screen |
| Resize | Pinch to resize (Android 12+) |
| Playback controls | Tap window for play/pause, close |
| Supported content | Local files, network streams, IPTV |

## ▶️ How to Enable Picture-in-Picture in VX Player

### Method 1: Press the Home Button During Playback

This is the simplest method:

1. Play any video in VX Player  
2. Press your device's **Home button** (the circular or rounded button at the bottom)  
3. VX Player automatically shrinks into a floating PiP window  
4. Navigate to any other app — the video continues in the corner  

> 💡 This works because VX Player has PiP mode configured to activate automatically when you leave the app. You don't need to do anything special — just press Home.

### Method 2: Use the PiP Button in the Player Controls

1. During playback, tap the screen to show controls  
2. Look for the **PiP icon** (a small rectangle with a miniature rectangle inside it) in the top bar  
3. Tap it — the video immediately goes into PiP mode  

### Method 3: From the Recent Apps Screen

1. During playback, tap the **Recent Apps / Square** button  
2. Long-press the VX Player card  
3. Some Android versions show a **PiP option** — tap it

## 🔧 Enabling PiP Permission on Android

If PiP doesn't work, your device may require explicit permission:

1. Go to **Android Settings**  
2. Navigate to **Apps → VX Player**  
3. Tap **Advanced** or **Special app access**  
4. Find **Picture-in-Picture**  
5. Toggle it **ON**  

On some devices (Samsung, Xiaomi, Oppo), this setting is located under:  
**Settings → Battery → App battery management → VX Player → Allow background activity**

> 💡 On Xiaomi/MIUI devices, you may also need to enable **"Show floating window"** permission in App permissions for VX Player.

## 🎮 Controlling the PiP Window

Once the floating window is active, here's how to interact with it:

### Move It
Drag the PiP window anywhere on the screen — corners, sides, or middle. Android snaps it to a comfortable position when you release.

### Resize It (Android 12+)
On Android 12 and later, you can pinch the PiP window to make it larger or smaller. Useful for content where detail matters.

### Pause / Play
Tap the PiP window once to reveal the mini controls overlay — a play/pause button appears in the centre.

### Close It
Tap the window to reveal controls, then tap the **✕ close button**. Or drag the window to the bottom of the screen — a trash/close area appears. Release over it to dismiss.

### Return to Full Screen
Tap the PiP window and tap the **expand / full-screen icon** to return VX Player to full-screen mode for the current video.

## 📐 PiP Window Positioning Best Practices

| Activity | Recommended PiP Position |
|---|---|
| Browsing social media | Top-right corner (away from feed) |
| Reading long articles | Bottom-right (away from text) |
| Messaging / WhatsApp | Top-left or top-right corner |
| Using navigation / Maps | Bottom of screen won't block map |
| Gaming | Avoid — PiP interferes with gameplay |

You can drag the window mid-stream to reposition it whenever the content underneath changes.

## 🚫 When PiP Doesn't Work — Known Limitations

| Limitation | Details |
|---|---|
| Android 7 and older | PiP is not supported on Android 7.x and below |
| DRM-protected content | Some DRM-encrypted streams disable PiP as a content protection measure |
| Screen recording while PiP | May cause the floating window to not appear in recordings |
| Full-screen game blocking | Some games draw over PiP windows; exit the game to see the video |
| Battery saver mode | Some OEM battery modes kill background apps including PiP |

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. What Android version is required for Picture-in-Picture in VX Player?
PiP mode requires Android 8.0 (Oreo) or later. If your device runs Android 7.x or earlier, PiP is not available at the OS level and no app can enable it. Most phones currently in use run Android 9 or newer.

### 2. Why doesn't VX Player automatically go into PiP when I press Home?
This usually means the PiP permission is not enabled for VX Player. Go to Settings → Apps → VX Player → Picture-in-Picture and enable it. On MIUI (Xiaomi), you may additionally need to allow "Display pop-up windows" in app permissions.

### 3. Can I use PiP mode while playing IPTV streams in VX Player?
Yes. PiP works with all content types in VX Player — local files, network streams, and IPTV M3U channels. Load your channel, start watching, and press Home to enter PiP mode.

### 4. Can I resize the PiP window in VX Player?
On Android 12 and later, you can pinch to resize the PiP window. On earlier Android versions, the PiP window size is fixed by the operating system. Newer Android versions allow more flexible control.

### 5. Does PiP mode affect battery life significantly?
PiP mode does consume slightly more battery than a black screen because the display remains active and video continues decoding. However, it uses far less battery than keeping VX Player full-screen. For long sessions, consider plugging in if PiP + other apps run simultaneously.
