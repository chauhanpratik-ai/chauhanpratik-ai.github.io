## 📥 How to Build Your Offline Video Library

### Step 1: Transfer Videos to Your Phone

**From a computer (USB cable):**
1. Connect your Android phone via USB  
2. Select **File Transfer (MTP)** mode on your phone  
3. Open File Explorer on PC → navigate to your phone's internal storage or SD card  
4. Copy video files into a folder like `Movies` or `Videos`  

**From a USB OTG flash drive:**
1. Connect a USB OTG adapter to your phone  
2. Plug in a flash drive loaded with videos  
3. Open VX Player → tap the 📁 Folder icon → navigate to the USB drive  
4. VX Player reads USB OTG storage directly — no copying needed  

**From a NAS or home network (while on Wi-Fi):**
1. Download files to internal storage while connected to Wi-Fi  
2. Once downloaded, they're available forever offline

## 🔋 Battery-Saving Tips for Offline Viewing

Long offline sessions — flights, camping trips, road journeys — demand efficient battery use. Here's how to maximise playback time:

**Enable Hardware Decoder (HW mode)**  
Hardware decoding uses the dedicated video chip rather than the CPU, consuming significantly less power. Verify this is enabled in Settings → Decoder → Hardware.

**Reduce screen brightness**  
Display is the biggest battery consumer. Reduce brightness to 50% or lower — in a dimly lit environment (plane, train at night), 30% is perfectly readable.

**Use background audio mode**  
For audio-heavy content, enable background playback and turn off the screen. VX Player continues delivering audio with lock-screen controls while the display is off, dramatically extending battery life.

**Close background apps**  
Before a long session, close all unnecessary apps. On devices with 4 GB RAM, this frees memory and prevents the OS from killing VX Player in the background.

**Enable Airplane Mode**  
When you have no need for connectivity, enabling Airplane Mode stops all radio transmissions (cellular, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth) and can extend battery life by 20–30%.

| SD card (UHS-I / Class 10) | Standard 1080p and below |

**Practical storage guide:**
- 1080p H.264 movie (2 hrs): ~4–8 GB  
- 1080p HEVC movie (2 hrs): ~2–4 GB  
- 4K HEVC movie (2 hrs): ~15–25 GB  
- SD card recommendation: 128–256 GB for a decent movie library

## 🚫 Common Offline Viewing Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Don't use a slow SD card for 4K.** Cards rated below Class 10 can't keep up with high-bitrate 4K reads, causing stuttering that has nothing to do with the app.

❌ **Don't keep VX Player open but sleep the phone without enabling background playback.** Some OEM launchers kill apps aggressively — enable background activity permission for VX Player in Android settings.

❌ **Don't forget subtitles.** Download and rename subtitle files before you go offline. Once offline, you can't download them from OpenSubtitles.

❌ **Don't store only H.264 files.** HEVC is ~50% smaller at the same quality — switching to HEVC re-encodes dramatically extends your storage capacity.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### 1. Does VX Player require an internet connection to work?
No. VX Player is a fully offline-capable video player. It plays local files stored on your device's internal storage, SD card, or USB OTG drive without any internet connection required. No account login is needed.

### 2. Can VX Player play videos from an SD card?
Yes. VX Player's built-in file manager browses both internal storage and SD card storage. Navigate to your SD card folder in the file manager and tap any video to play it directly.

### 3. What is the best video format for offline storage on Android?
H.265/HEVC in an MP4 or MKV container offers the best quality-to-size ratio for offline storage. It's roughly 50% smaller than H.264 at equivalent quality, letting you store twice as many videos in the same space.

### 4. Can I create playlists in VX Player for offline binge-watching?
Yes. VX Player supports playlist creation from the file manager. Long-press any video, add it to a new or existing playlist, and queue up an entire TV series or movie marathon for uninterrupted playback.

### 5. How much storage do I need for a good offline movie library?
A 128 GB SD card or internal storage can hold approximately 20–30 movies in 1080p HEVC format. If you prefer 4K, plan for 5–8 movies per 128 GB. A 256 GB SD card (very affordable in 2026) gives comfortable headroom for a travel library.
